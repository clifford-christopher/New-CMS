IDFC First Bank Rating Downgraded from 'Buy' to 'Hold': Quality Grade Deterioration Triggers Score Change

Mumbai | 28 October 2025 | By Investment Research Desk

IDFC First Bank Ltd. has seen its investment rating downgraded from 'Buy' to 'Hold' by MarketsMOJO on 23 October 2025, following a deterioration in its quality assessment that pushed the bank's overall score down to 64 from the buy threshold. The downgrade comes as the bank's quality grade slipped from 'Good' to 'Average', reflecting concerns about long-term fundamental performance despite continued positive near-term financial trends.

The rating change represents a significant shift in investment stance for one of India's mid-cap private sector banks, currently trading at ₹79.20 with a market capitalisation of ₹64,563.50 crores. For investors holding IDFC First Bank stock, this downgrade signals a time for cautious evaluation rather than aggressive accumulation, whilst prospective buyers may want to wait for clearer quality improvements before initiating positions.

Understanding the Quality Grade Change: What Triggered the Downgrade

The primary catalyst for IDFC First Bank's rating downgrade was a fundamental shift in its quality assessment. The bank's quality grade, which evaluates long-term fundamental performance over a five-year period, deteriorated from 'Good' to 'Average' on 27 October 2025. This quality parameter is one of four critical components that determine the overall MOJO Score, alongside valuation, financial trend, and technical factors.

The quality downgrade is particularly significant because it reflects sustained fundamental performance rather than short-term fluctuations. Whilst the bank maintains an impressive 25.09% compound annual growth rate in net profits over five years and a robust 27.03% growth in net interest income, certain underlying quality metrics have shown deterioration that warranted the grade revision.

Parameter Changes Behind the Score Revision

Quality Grade: Changed from Good to Average on 27-Oct-2025

Impact: This single parameter change pushed the overall score from the 'Buy' category (70-80 range) down to the 'Hold' category (50-70 range) at 64 points.

Other Parameters: Financial Trend remains Positive, Technicals remain Bullish, and Valuation remains Very Expensive—all unchanged but insufficient to maintain Buy rating without quality support.

The quality assessment considers multiple factors including consistency of earnings, balance sheet strength, and operational efficiency over extended periods. For IDFC First Bank, whilst growth metrics remain strong, the bank's return on equity of just 3.15% and return on assets of 0.38% in the latest quarter represent significant underperformance relative to established private sector bank peers.

Financial Performance Analysis: Strengths and Weaknesses

IDFC First Bank's September 2025 quarter demonstrated continued operational momentum with net interest income reaching an all-time high of ₹5,112.56 crores, representing 6.78% year-on-year growth. Interest earned climbed to ₹9,936.89 crores, whilst the bank maintained a healthy CASA ratio of 50.10%, indicating strong retail franchise strength.

However, profitability metrics reveal underlying challenges. Net profit for the quarter stood at ₹352.31 crores, down 23.84% sequentially from ₹462.57 crores in the previous quarter. More concerning is the nine-month profit performance, which declined 30.31% year-on-year to ₹1,118.96 crores, highlighting pressure on bottom-line growth despite top-line expansion.

Valuation Concerns: Trading at Stretched Multiples

IDFC First Bank's valuation assessment of 'Very Expensive' compounds the quality concerns and contributes significantly to the Hold rating. The bank trades at a price-to-earnings ratio of 44x on a trailing twelve-month basis, substantially above the private sector bank peer average of approximately 21x and nearly three times the multiple of established players like Axis Bank (16x) or IDBI Bank (10.74x).

The valuation grade has progressively deteriorated through 2025, moving from 'Attractive' in April to 'Fair' and then 'Expensive' before reaching 'Very Expensive' status on 27 October 2025. This trajectory suggests the market has been pricing in growth expectations that the bank's current quality metrics may not support.

Asset Quality and Capital Adequacy: Mixed Signals

IDFC First Bank's asset quality metrics present a nuanced picture that contributes to the quality assessment challenges. Gross non-performing assets improved to 1.86% in September 2025, the lowest level in recent quarters, whilst net NPAs remained contained at 0.52%. These figures compare favourably to historical levels and suggest effective credit risk management.

However, the provision coverage ratio declined to 72.20% from 75.27% a year earlier, indicating reduced buffer against potential credit losses. More significantly, provisions and contingencies totalled ₹5,514 crores for fiscal 2025, more than doubling from ₹2,381 crores in the previous year, which directly impacted profitability and contributed to the 48.4% decline in annual net profit.

The stock trades above all key moving averages—5-day (₹77.78), 20-day (₹73.03), 50-day (₹71.67), 100-day (₹71.85), and 200-day (₹66.96)—indicating strong price momentum.

The technical trend turned decisively bullish on 20 October 2025 at ₹76.91, supported by positive MACD signals on both weekly and monthly timeframes, bullish Bollinger Bands, and strong on-balance volume trends. The stock has delivered impressive returns of 15.45% over one month and 25.42% year-to-date, substantially outperforming the Sensex's 5.22% and 8.30% returns respectively.

For Current Holders: Strategic Considerations

Investors currently holding IDFC First Bank stock face a nuanced decision following the rating downgrade to Hold. The recommendation is clear: continue holding existing positions but avoid adding to positions at current levels. This stance reflects the balance between ongoing business momentum and valuation-quality concerns.

Current holders benefit from the strong year-to-date return of 25.42% and should recognise that the Hold rating does not suggest immediate exit. The bank's operational metrics remain sound, with deposit growth of 25.65% year-on-year, advances growth of 19.80%, and improving asset quality. The positive financial trend grade and bullish technical indicators provide near-term support for the stock price.

For Prospective Buyers: Wait for Better Entry Points

Investors considering initiating positions in IDFC First Bank should exercise patience following the rating downgrade. The Hold rating explicitly states the stock is "not recommended for fresh buy" at current levels of ₹79.20, reflecting unfavourable risk-reward dynamics given the quality concerns and stretched valuation.

The primary issue for prospective buyers is the valuation-quality mismatch. Paying 44x earnings for a bank generating just 3.15% return on equity and classified as 'Average' quality creates an asymmetric risk profile. Even if the bank delivers on growth expectations, the elevated multiple limits upside potential, whilst any disappointment could trigger meaningful multiple compression.

Growth Trajectory and Long-Term Outlook

Despite near-term quality concerns, IDFC First Bank's long-term growth trajectory remains intact, supported by India's underpenetrated banking market and the bank's focus on retail and small business segments. The five-year compound annual growth rate of 25.09% in net profits and 27.03% in net interest income demonstrates the franchise's fundamental expansion capability.

The bank's deposit base grew from ₹65,107.97 crores in March 2020 to ₹2,52,065.25 crores in March 2025, representing a remarkable 31.15% compound annual growth rate. Advances expanded from ₹85,595.36 crores to ₹2,33,112.53 crores over the same period, a 22.23% CAGR, indicating strong loan book momentum that should support future earnings growth.

Risk Factors and Monitoring Points

Several key risk factors warrant close monitoring following the rating downgrade. The compression in net interest margins from 6.35% in March 2024 to 5.60% currently represents a 75 basis point decline that directly impacts profitability. If this trend continues below 5.50%, it would signal intensifying competitive pressures that could further constrain earnings growth.

The elevated provisions and contingencies remain a concern. The ₹5,514 crores provisioned in fiscal 2025 represented more than double the previous year's ₹2,381 crores, consuming significant operating profit. Whilst asset quality metrics show improvement, the provision coverage ratio declining to 72.20% from historical levels above 75% suggests potential vulnerability to credit cycles.

Conclusion: Navigating the Rating Change

The downgrade of IDFC First Bank from Buy to Hold following its quality grade deterioration from Good to Average represents a significant reassessment of the bank's investment merit. The rating change, triggered on 23 October 2025, reflects fundamental concerns about long-term operational efficiency and profitability generation despite continued business momentum.

For current holders, the Hold rating suggests maintaining positions whilst monitoring quarterly performance for signs of quality improvement or further deterioration. The positive financial trend and bullish technical indicators provide near-term support, but the quality concerns and stretched valuation at 44x earnings limit aggressive accumulation at current levels.

For prospective buyers, the rating downgrade and Very Expensive valuation assessment create an unfavourable entry point. Patience is warranted, with potential entry opportunities emerging either through price correction to the ₹70-72 range or fundamental improvement demonstrated through sustained ROE enhancement and quality grade upgrade.

The investment thesis for IDFC First Bank ultimately hinges on management's ability to translate strong growth metrics into improved profitability and operational efficiency. Until the quality grade improves back to Good status and return on equity trends towards double digits, the Hold rating appropriately reflects the balanced risk-reward profile facing investors in this mid-cap private sector bank.
